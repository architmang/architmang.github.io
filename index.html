About me

The story so far

I build production camera software at Samsung Research Bangalore that ships on Galaxy S-series flagships. My work spans 60 fps stabilized video pipelines, 8K HDR capture, and ARM SIMD/OpenCL optimizations running at sub-millisecond latency.

Before Samsung, I did research stints at the University of Alberta (MITACS Scholar, 2023) building multimodal motion capture systems, and at TU Dresden (Erasmus+ Scholar, 2022) working on approximate computing for FPGA neural network inference.

I'm drawn to the layer of software where math meets metal. Whether that's a Kalman filter tracking hedge ratios at 10 ms or a quantized neural net running on a phone's NPU, I care about making things fast, correct, and shipped.
